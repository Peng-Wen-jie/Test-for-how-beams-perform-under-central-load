Background

Investigation of how beams made by different materials with various geometry features perform under central load

July 30, 2020 Peng Wenjie

I prepared an experiment of simply supported beam in order to find out how different materials performed under pressure.

Firstly, measuring data (length between two fulcrums, thickness of solid part, h and b). During the experiment, use the digital dial indicator to measure the variables of the rods.

Second, make measurements. As Fig 4 shows, beams will be placed on two supports and then load a force F acting in the midpoint of it and vertically downward. The load will be imposed by adding weights (10N for each time and not less than 4 times for accurate results). After adding load each time, read the number displayed and take record. Test the deformation for each rod.

Finally, calculate the theoretical value of each rods under different load using the formulae derived in Appendix 1. Make a form contains both theoretical and real value. After that, draw some diagrams in order to compare the difference more obvious.
