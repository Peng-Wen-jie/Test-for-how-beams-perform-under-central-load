Abstract

Investigation of how beams made by different materials with various geometry features perform under central load

July 30, 2020 Peng Wenjie

This project concerns the research and implementations of Young’s modulus of elasticity, relationships between curvature, rotation and deflection and introduce the basic concepts of structural and material engineering through a beam loading test. In the experiment, I use weights to apply a load at the centre of the beam and measure the resulting mid-span deflection. After finding the Young’s Modulus value and calculated the second moment of inertia value for beam of particular material and cross-section geometry, I calculated the theoretical central deflection value for each beam under ascending load. This experiment provides data for discussing material engineering topics, such as elastic and inelastic behaviour, the influence of materials and structural shapes, stiffness and strength.
